13 Notes to Balloters

This section is to be move to the front for ballot to request comments on specific sections from balloters.

13.1 Slicing discriminator limited to primitive components

By choice the slicing discriminator is limited primitive components. Reasoning included:
Below are a few examples of how subcomponent discriminators could be used:
Example 1:
RXA|0|1|20150625||58160-0820-01^ENGERIX-B^NDC|0.5|mL^mL^UCUM||00^New Record^NIP001|7824^Jackson^Lily^Suzanne^^^^^NIST-PI-1&2.16.840.1.113883.3.72.5.40.7&ISO^L^^^PRN|^^^NIST-Clinic-1&2.16.840.1.113883.3.72.5.40.12&ISO||||797397|20151203|SKB^GlaxoSmithKline^MVX|||CP|A
If someone wanted to slice the Administering Provider in RXA-10 by the provider ID Assigning Authority (RXA-10.9) they could do so by making all 3 sub-components of RXA-10.9 as a discriminator (“NIST-PI-1&2.16.840.1.113883.3.72.5.40.7&ISO”). Note, the discriminator would be 3 related individual discriminators in the definition.
Example 2:
Here only a single sub-component is used as the discriminator (RXA-10.9.1).
